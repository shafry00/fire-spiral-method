# Case Study #1: Drumming — Physical Skill

> **Zone: Hybrid** | WANT: 8.0 | PUSH: 5.5 | Drive Score: 0.42

## AMATI

### Problem Statement

"The problem is developing 4-limb coordination to play drum patterns that require simultaneous independence. This matters because without it, I can't play full songs — only isolated patterns."

## PRAKTEKKAN — 3 Sessions

### Session 1: Baseline Assessment

**Goal:** Try to play a simple 4/4 beat with right hand (hi-hat), left hand (snare), right foot (kick), left foot (hi-hat pedal).

**Gap Log:**

| # | Gap |
|---|-----|
| 1 | **Thought:** Right hand on auto-pilot from years of habit. **Reality:** Adding left hand throws right hand timing off. **Insight:** Independence is not "doing multiple things at once" — it's training each limb to operate without the others as reference. |
| 2 | **Thought:** Start slow, gradually speed up. **Reality:** Slow tempo is actually harder — no momentum, no groove feel to latch onto. **Insight:** Medium tempo (80-100 BPM) is the comfort zone. Extremes (very slow or very fast) require different techniques. |

### Session 2: Targeted Drills

**Goal:** Isolate left hand independence — practice snare patterns while maintaining hi-hat.

**Gap Log:**

| # | Gap |
|---|-----|
| 3 | **Thought:** Practice each limb separately until muscle memory, then combine. **Reality:** Combining throws everything off — the coordination is not additive, it's multiplicative. **Insight:** Need to practice transitions between combinations, not just each combination in isolation. |
| 4 | **Thought:** Physical stamina isn't the bottleneck — it's mental coordination. **Reality:** After 20 minutes of focused 4-limb practice, arms feel heavy and timing degrades. **Insight:** Physical endurance IS the hidden bottleneck. Mental coordination depletes physical energy faster than expected. |

### Session 3: Full Song Attempt

**Goal:** Play through a full song (verse + chorus + bridge).

**Gap Log:**

| # | Gap |
|---|-----|
| 5 | **Thought:** If I can play each section separately, I can chain them. **Reality:** Transitions between sections cause timing breaks — each section has different limb patterns and switching creates a reset lag. **Insight:** Section transitions are a separate skill that needs dedicated practice — not just practicing sections. |
| 6 | **Thought:** Metronome is for beginners. **Reality:** Without metronome, tempo drifts across sections — verse at 95 BPM, chorus speeds to 105 BPM. **Insight:** Metronome is not training wheels, it's a verification instrument. Like a compiler for code. |

### Proof Anchor

| Gate | Target | Result |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Gate 1 — Reproduce** | Play verse+chorus without stopping | Partial — can play each section, transitions still break |
| **Gate 2 — Explain** | Explain 4-limb coordination to someone else | Yes — can articulate that "independence = training without reference" |
| **Gate 3 — Break** | Identify where timing breaks and why | Yes — identified transitions as the specific failure point |

**Proof Anchor: 2/3.** Gate 1 was the bottleneck.

## Key Insight

**Skill issue ≠ guidance issue.** The initial framing ("I need to learn 4-limb coordination") was wrong. The real problem was: stamina management during focused coordination, section transitions as a discrete skill, and metronome as verification not training wheels.

This case study validated the **problem deepening** claim — each spiral revealed a more fundamental layer than the previous one imagined.
